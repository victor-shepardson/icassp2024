Towards Real-time Interactive Neural Text-to-Voice Synthesis for Artistic Exploration

Victor Shepardson and Jonathan Chaim Reus

Abstract:

In recent years, deep learning has enabled speech synthesis to rival human speech in realism. While artists have experimented with these technologies, real-time interactive applications have been limited. To bridge this gap, we present a novel interactive text-to-voice (ITTV) system which achieves low-latency synthesis, interactivity, and controllability while exploring unconventional vocal expressions. It leverages a character-level text encoder, Tacotron2-based streaming alignment, and a RAVE VAE as a novel streaming vocoder. The results demonstrate that our architecture successfully marries the unique aesthetics of DNN-based voice synthesis with real-time control, addressing artistic requirements not well addressed in current research and paving the way for future work.
